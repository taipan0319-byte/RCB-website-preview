## 1. The assignment

Create a concise, sophisticated website for Rockford Consulting & Brokerage (RCB), an independent consulting and brokerage firm working across employee benefits, pharmacy arrangements, employer health, risk, retirement plans and management systems.

The website is not intended to be a comprehensive institutional archive. Its principal jobs are to:

1. Confirm that RCB is an established, credible and technically excellent firm.
2. Make the firm's address, telephone number and email immediately accessible.
3. Give prospective clients confidence that RCB can produce meaningful results in difficult, high-consequence situations.
4. Create enough interest for a decision-maker to contact RCB.

The site should communicate depth without publishing personnel profiles, client lists or excessive operational detail.

## 3. Desired market impression

The visitor should leave with four impressions:

- **RCB produces results.** It does not merely study problems or offer generic advice.
- **RCB understands complexity.** It can follow financial, contractual, clinical and organizational relationships that others miss.
- **RCB is independent and disciplined.** Its recommendations are driven by evidence and outcomes.
- **RCB sees greater possibilities.** A health plan can improve health rather than simply finance disease and injury; management information can change performance rather than merely document it.

The intended personality is a combination of:

- the stability and discretion of a respected financial-services firm;
- the rigor of a technically excellent consulting practice;
- the curiosity of an investigator;
- the optimism of a firm that believes entrenched problems can be changed.

Avoid making RCB appear to be an engineering company, software startup, advertising agency or generic insurance brokerage.

## 4. Current draft: what is working

The live draft establishes a useful foundation:

- Warm ivory, deep navy and heritage blue feel established and restrained.
- Editorial typography differentiates RCB from ordinary brokerage websites.
- The opening invitation—**“Bring us your toughest challenges”**—is approachable and relevant.
- The animated visual expresses complexity moving toward direction.
- The wellness proposition introduces a larger ambition than conventional benefits consulting.
- Contact information is prominent.
- The site avoids client names and personnel listings.

These elements should be treated as starting assets, not untouchable decisions.

## 5. Current draft: what needs to improve

### Strategic issue

Much of the current language says, in effect:

- we look closely;
- we ask questions;
- we examine complexity;
- we help clients find a path.

Those are credible methods, but they are not the ultimate promise. The next version should place greater emphasis on what changes because RCB is involved:

- hidden value is recovered;
- vendor obligations become accountable;
- costs are brought under control;
- health improves;
- disease can be reversed or better managed;
- operating decisions become evidence-based;
- financial performance strengthens;
- persistent problems become better outcomes.

**Creative correction:** Effort is the method. Outcomes are the promise.

The language must remain supportable and sophisticated. Avoid guarantees, inflated superlatives and claims that cannot be documented.

## 6. Positioning territories to explore

These are creative territories for comparison, not approved final copy.

### Territory A — Difficult problems, meaningful results

**Strategic idea:** RCB is most valuable where conventional answers have failed or important details have been missed.

Possible headlines:

- **Bring us the challenge. Expect a better outcome.**
- **Difficult problems deserve consequential results.**
- **When the problem is complex, the result matters more.**
- **Turn the issue no one has solved into the result everyone needs.**
- **Complexity is not the outcome. Progress is.**

Tone: direct, confident and broadly applicable across all RCB services.

### Territory B — What complexity is costing you

**Strategic idea:** Complexity often conceals waste, weak accountability, misaligned incentives and unrealized opportunity.

Possible headlines:

- **Find what complexity is costing you.**
- **Complexity hides more than risk. It hides opportunity.**
- **What others overlook can change the outcome.**
- **See the system. Change the result.**
- **Hidden in the details: a better result.**

Tone: analytical and provocative. Particularly strong for pharmacy review, audits and vendor contracts.

### Territory C — Health plans that improve health

**Strategic idea:** Employers should demand more from a health plan than the financing of treatment after disease or injury occurs.

Possible headlines:

- **What if your health plan actually made people healthier?**
- **Improve health. Change the economics.**
- **A health plan should produce health—not just pay for treatment.**
- **Better health is a better financial strategy.**
- **Move beyond financing disease. Start changing outcomes.**

Tone: optimistic, human and differentiated. It must be framed as an employer strategy, not a promise of individual medical treatment or a “wellness program.”

### Territory D — Independent judgment, accountable performance

**Strategic idea:** RCB connects evidence, independent analysis and implementation to create measurable performance.

Possible headlines:

- **Clear evidence. Decisive action. Measurable results.**
- **Advice should change the result.**
- **Independent thinking. Accountable outcomes.**
- **From evidence to action—from action to results.**

Tone: steady, institutional and appropriate for public entities and financial decision-makers.

### Recommended direction for the next concept round

Develop one concept from **Territory A/B** and one from **Territory C**:

1. A broad firm-level concept built around difficult problems producing meaningful results.
2. A health-led concept built around the possibility that health plans can improve health and financial performance.

The broad concept is the safer master brand. The health-led concept may be the more emotionally compelling alternative.

## 7. Provisional message hierarchy

### Opening

Lead with the desired transformation or outcome. Follow with a concise explanation of how RCB creates it.

**Example structure:**

> **Bring us the challenge. Expect a better outcome.**
>
> RCB transforms complex health plans, pharmacy arrangements and operating systems into stronger performance—finding hidden value, enforcing accountability and creating better paths forward.

This example is intentionally more result-oriented than the present draft but still requires refinement.

### Firm-level proposition

RCB works where contracts, incentives, claims, vendors, health outcomes and operating data intersect. The firm understands the entire system, identifies what is suppressing performance and helps implement a better result.

### Health proposition

> **A health plan should do more than pay for sickness.**

When primary care, pharmacy strategy, plan design and timely data work as one system, employers can improve health outcomes, help reverse chronic disease and reduce avoidable long-term cost.

### Proof proposition

RCB's pharmacy diligence helped identify extraordinary costs and vendor issues involving H.P. Acthar Gel. The point is not simply that RCB found an expensive drug. The work demonstrates the ability to understand complicated pharmacy benefit design, contracts, pricing, incentives, claims and vendor relationships—and to recognize when the experience does not make sense.

### Closing invitation

The closing should be more purposeful than “Contact us.” Consider:

- **Start with the result you need.**
- **Tell us what needs to change.**
- **Bring us the issue that matters.**
- **Let’s define a better outcome.**

## 8. Recommended page structure

The site should remain a focused one-page experience unless the designer demonstrates a compelling need for secondary pages.

1. **Header** — RCB mark and live company name; Expertise, Approach, Selected Work and Contact; visible telephone number.
2. **Outcome-led hero** — headline, short supporting statement, primary action and signature motion/3D visual.
3. **Outcome statement** — what changes because RCB is involved.
4. **Expertise** — pharmacy review and audit; employee benefits and risk; employer health and onsite care; management/performance systems; retirement and pensions.
5. **Health philosophy** — the emotional and strategic contrast between paying for treatment and improving health.
6. **Selected work** — carefully framed Acthar example and, if substantiated, municipal financial/performance recognition.
7. **Operating principle** — independence, evidence, implementation and accountability.
8. **Contact** — complete address, phone and email; no unnecessary lead-capture friction.

## 9. Visual direction

### Overall system

- Warm white or ivory dominant background.
- Deep ink navy for authority and contrast.
- Heritage/process blue used with restraint.
- Editorial serif for major statements, paired with a disciplined modern sans serif.
- Generous negative space.
- Fine rules and precise alignment.
- Few visual elements, each with a clear purpose.
- Motion concentrated in one signature moment rather than scattered throughout the page.

The page should look expensive because it is controlled, not because it is busy.

- **BCG AI:** one cinematic motion object surrounded by quiet institutional design; strong contrast and controlled pacing.

## 10. Signature 3D/motion brief

### Current concept

The current hero shows tangled blue energy becoming a focused beam. The metaphor is directionally correct: RCB turns complexity into clarity. However, the existing treatment can feel like a two-dimensional particle visualization or an AI/technology graphic. It does not yet fully communicate the production of a result.

### Required evolution

The next visual should show a completed transformation—not merely analysis in progress.

The sequence should have a clear narrative:

1. **Complex conditions enter:** multiple translucent layers, filaments, signals or contractual/data structures coexist without obvious order.
2. **Underlying relationships become visible:** elements react, align and reveal a governing structure.
3. **A material result emerges:** the system resolves into a stable, precise and visibly stronger form, path or field.

Motion should feel inevitable and controlled, as if intelligence reveals the solution already latent in the system.

### Visual concepts to explore

#### Option 1 — The resolved structure

Translucent sheets or ribbons with fine contractual/data markings intersect in three-dimensional space. As the viewpoint shifts, apparent disorder resolves into a precise continuous structure. This suggests understanding the whole system rather than simply untangling a knot.

#### Option 2 — From friction to flow

A dense field of blue filaments meets resistance, redistributes and emerges as a coherent, wider flow. The end state should look more valuable and capable—not merely narrower. This avoids implying that RCB's only result is simplification.

#### Option 3 — The outcome field

Scattered points and relationships progressively form a stable dimensional field or surface. Specific areas strengthen and illuminate as evidence aligns. This works well for data-driven management but must not resemble engineering simulation software.

#### Option 4 — Health restored

An abstract living network begins fragmented or stressed and becomes more connected, rhythmic and resilient. Avoid literal anatomy, DNA, medical devices, pills or hospital imagery. The visual should suggest vitality without making a medical claim.

### Recommended visual test

Develop **Option 1** and **Option 4** as contrasting storyboards:

- Option 1 represents RCB's firm-wide analytical and contractual capability.
- Option 4 tests whether the health proposition can create a more human emotional response.

### Art-direction constraints

- Sophisticated dimensional depth, refraction, translucency and controlled light.
- Heritage blue, cobalt and pale cyan against deep navy or near-black.
- No gears, machines, lenses, funnels, circuit boards, dashboards or literal documents.
- No explosive energy, aggressive speed or science-fiction interface language.
- No generic AI “neural network” sphere.
- No visual that suggests an engineering, architecture or software-development firm.
- Avoid excessive mouse-follow effects; subtle parallax is acceptable.
- Six-to-eight-second seamless motion cycle.
- First frame must be attractive before animation begins.
- Static fallback for mobile and slow connections.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`; provide a visible pause control.
- The page headline and contact information must load before the animation.

### Evaluation question

Do not ask only, “Does it look impressive?” Ask:

> **Does the visual make the viewer feel that RCB produces a stronger result from a difficult system?**

## 11. Expertise content guidance

### Pharmacy contract review and audit

Focus on outcomes such as finding leakage, recovering value, testing guarantees, improving contract terms and making vendors accountable. Explain that contract language and actual claim experience must be examined together.

Avoid presenting this as a routine checklist audit.

### Employee benefits and risk strategy

Connect plan design, procurement, financing, service and vendor performance to the organization's larger financial and workforce objectives.

### Employer health and onsite care

Present onsite and near-site care as part of a disciplined health strategy, not as a workplace amenity. Emphasize access, earlier intervention, chronic-condition improvement, integrated pharmacy strategy, measurable outcomes and long-term economics.

### Management and performance systems

Explain that RCB designs operating systems that replace anecdote with measurable priorities, regular executive review and accountability. A stat-style management system may be referenced without naming the client.

### Retirement and pension consulting

Emphasize fiduciary discipline, sound plan design, vendor performance and practical administration.

## 13. Content and tone rules

### Use

- active verbs tied to change: improve, recover, strengthen, reduce, enforce, reverse, transform, produce;
- short, declarative sentences;
- specific explanations of how results are created;
- confident language proportionate to the evidence;
- plain English for technically complicated services.

### Avoid

- generic phrases such as “innovative solutions,” “trusted partner,” “leveraging expertise” and “best in class”;
- repeated statements about working hard, looking closely or helping clients navigate;
- excessive insurance, pharmacy or management jargon;
- an adversarial tone that suggests every vendor is acting improperly;
- promises or guarantees that exceed documented evidence;
- copy that makes RCB appear larger than it is rather than more capable than expected.

## 14. Technical direction

Version one should remain technically simple:

- Static or mostly static implementation using a modern framework such as Astro or Next.js.
- No CMS unless a genuine publishing need emerges.
- No personnel database or client portal.
- Fast first render and no animation dependency for understanding the page.
- Responsive from 320 pixels through large desktop displays.
- Accessible keyboard navigation and visible focus states.
- Meaningful headings and appropriate structured business data.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` support and animation pause control.
- Production targets: LCP ≤ 2.5 seconds, INP ≤ 200 milliseconds and CLS ≤ 0.1.
- Exact contact information visible in rendered HTML.
- Search-engine title, description, canonical URL, Organization/LocalBusiness structured data, sitemap and robots file.
- No trackers at launch unless specifically approved.

2. Desktop and mobile compositions for each concept.

## 16. Questions for a creative or web designer

1. Which outcome can RCB credibly own that competitors are not already claiming?
2. Does the firm-wide or health-led proposition create more desire without narrowing RCB too much?
3. How can the 3D object show that a result has been produced, rather than that analysis is merely underway?
4. Is the current editorial serif appropriate, or does it make the site feel too literary?
5. Can the historical RCB mark be integrated in a way that feels established rather than dated?
6. What should be removed so the site becomes more confident, not merely shorter?
7. Where should proof appear so it strengthens the promise without turning the page into a case-study archive?

2. Should the wellness philosophy be the site's emotional centerpiece or the first major section beneath a broader firm promise?
3. Should the hero say “Bring us your toughest challenges,” or should it lead directly with a result?
4. Which 3D metaphor best communicates a completed result?
5. How much detail should be published about Acthar after legal and factual review?

## 19. Success test

Within approximately ten seconds, a first-time visitor should understand:

- what kind of firm RCB is;
- that RCB works on difficult, consequential business and health-plan problems;
- that RCB's value is measured in changed outcomes, not activity;
- that the firm is credible, independent and technically sophisticated;
- how to contact RCB.

The ultimate test is the thought the site should create:

> **“We have a difficult issue. These are the people who may be able to change the result.”**
